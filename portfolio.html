§ 01 — About

From software foundations
to applied AI research.

sukkur, pakistan

I'm a fresh Computer Science graduate making the deliberate move from full-stack software engineering into AI/ML research and applied development. My early work — Java and MySQL banking systems, college management platforms, React-based web apps — built the engineering discipline that now backs every model I ship: clean architecture, reliable data handling, and software that survives contact with real users.

That foundation is now pointed squarely at deep learning. I've benchmarked and fine-tuned Vision-Language Models (LLaVA, CogVLM, Qwen-VL) for real-world accessibility use cases, built LoRA-tuned assistive narration systems, shipped RAG-based conversational pipelines, and trained classifiers spanning speaker recognition, emotion detection, and healthcare prediction. My approach stays research-disciplined — literature review, dataset curation, rigorous benchmarking, statistical evaluation — but always aimed at a working, deployable result.

That discipline earned an accepted paper presentation at iCOMET 2026 (IEEE Xplore, forthcoming), on benchmarking VLMs for visually impaired assistance — work I'm continuing to extend through the SceneNarrator project below.
